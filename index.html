About Me

Welcome to my website!

I am a theoretical physics PhD student in the University of Tübingen specializing in ultracold many-body optics, Rydberg atoms, and open quantum systems. My research focuses on the interplay of light-matter interaction in highly controlled environments, exploring novel quantum phenomena and their potential applications in quantum technologies.

Research Interests

  • Ultracold atoms and quantum many-body systems
  • Rydberg atoms in superconducting cavities
  • Competing ranged interactions
  • Simulation of quantum systems

Recent Projects

Engineering strong light-matter interactions in thermal atom-cavity systems

Studying how internal spin structure and motional degrees of freedom can be tuned to enhance light-matter interactions and generate nontrivial correlations.

View on GitHub ArXiv link

Neutral atoms for multiqubit gates

Exploring the applications of collective Rydberg excitations to engineer many-body gates and complex effective spin systems.

View on GitHub

Numerical Methods for Many-Body Quantum Systems

Developing numerical simulations for solving complex many-body quantum systems within a quantum and semiclassical framework. Discrete and continuous Truncated Wigner approximation, Matrix Product States and Mean-Field methods.
